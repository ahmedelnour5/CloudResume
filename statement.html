Statement of Interdisciplinarity

I am Ahmed Elnour, an Interdisciplinary Studies Major, with a concentration in Math and Economics.My major allowed me to design a personalized degree which fit my future aspirations.

Coming into college I wanted to major in Computer Science. I thought Computer Science would allow me to reach my goal of building my own startup one day. I realized that this was an unrealistic goal considering the millions of people I would be competing against. However, I also realized I could also become successful by joining a startup.

There are many areas within a startup where one could thrive besides coding. These areas include operations, marketing, product, and finance. So I started to identify skills which would put me on a trajectory to thrive within one of these areas. Ultimately, I figured economics and math would get the job done.

Math is the foundation of all our sciences. The discipline covers a plethora of topics. The branches relevant to business are calculus and statistics. Calculus is the study of finding the rate at which different functions change. Statistics is the study of collecting and analyzing data. Finding the rate of change, and collecting and analyzing data, are some of the job requirements of an Economist.

Economics is the study of how scarce resources are allocated, and how individuals make decisions based on scarce resources. An economist performs these studies by performing marginal analysis. This analysis compares the benefits of taking an action with the costs associated with taking the action. A business leader must perform the same analysis in order to become successful.

Behind the scenes, a successful business performs lots of calculations and analyses. The business must make decisions about how to allocate scarce resources like time, labor, money, and factors of production. Business leaders and key personnel must have a solid understanding of math and economics to perform these tasks well.

An interdisciplinary degree combining math and economics provided me the best opportunity to learn these vital business skills. Integrating these disciplines will allow me to understand complex problems from the viewpoints of these lens. In addition, my interdisciplinary studies instilled me with values of empathy, humility, and ethical conscious.

Empathy is the ability to deeply understand the thoughts, feelings, and worldview of other humans. Humility is the ability to honestly define and assess your own skills and abilities. Ethical conscious is the awareness of the effects of your actions. I will always strive to practice these values professionally and personally.
